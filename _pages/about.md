Hi! I’m Ginny, and I am a quantitative researcher excited about uncovering meaningful, nuanced insights from complex data. I have 8 years of experience working on interdisciplinary research projects from their earliest stages (experimental and survey design, analysis planning) through completion (data visualization, synthesis, and communication of findings), and have worked in corporate UXR/consumer insights, academic psychology, and business school research settings.

I hold a Ph.D. in Psychology with a concentration in Quantitative Methods from Temple University, where my academic research explored how everyday forms of social connection are associated with emotional well-being and ways that social cognition can relate to pursuit of personal and shared goals. I also hold a B.S. from Tufts University, where I majored in Psychology, Spanish, and International Relations.
